01

Lock down your privacy first

Before sharing anything personal with an AI, go to Settings and turn off conversation training. On ChatGPT: Settings → Data Controls → disable "Improve the model for everyone." On Grok: Settings → Privacy. This stops your conversations from being used to train future AI models.

Once that's done, what you share stays between you and the AI. Treat it like a private notebook. The more honest you are, the more useful it becomes, but you don't have to go deep on day one. Start with the areas that feel safe. Depth builds naturally.

02

What is a prompt

A prompt is the instruction you give the AI. Most people write one vague sentence and wonder why the output is mediocre. A well-built prompt has four parts. The difference in quality is enormous.

Role
Tell it who to be
A financial advisor? A language coach? A family therapist? The role shapes everything: vocabulary, depth, angle of questioning.
Context
Give it the background
Who you are, your situation, your constraints, your history. The more specific, the better the advice.
Command
Tell it what to do
Be precise. "Help me think through this" is weak. "Interview me about X and identify my blind spots" is actionable.
Format
Tell it how to deliver
A PDF? A bullet list? A structured document with sections? Specify it or you'll get a wall of text.

Tip: At the end of any session, ask the AI: "What prompt would have gotten us to this result faster?" It will tell you exactly what you should have said upfront. Do this a few times and your prompting instinct sharpens quickly. Notice that this is itself a pull move: you're making the AI extract better practice from the interaction.

03

Push vs pull prompting

This is the most important mindset shift in this guide. Most people push information at the AI and ask it to finish the job. There's a better way: make the AI pull the information out of you first.

Push prompting
You do 80% of the work
You write a long brief, explain everything upfront, then ask AI to complete the last 20%. Exhausting. Often incomplete because you don't know what you've left out.
Pull prompting ✦
AI does the heavy lifting
You give it a role and an objective. It interviews you, extracts what it needs, then does the work. You just answer questions.

The pull workflow, which this entire guide is built around, looks like this:

  • 1Give it a role and context, who it is, who you are
  • 2Give it an outcome-based objective, what you need at the end
  • 3Ask it to interview you about everything it needs to know, one question at a time
  • 4Answer the questions, use voice mode if you can, it's faster and more natural
  • 5Ask it to do the work and deliver in your chosen format (PDF, document, structured plan)

Voice mode tip: in ChatGPT, tap the headphone icon to switch to voice. Let the AI read its questions aloud, answer naturally, then switch back to text for the final output. The interview flows much better spoken than typed.

04

Map your life areas

Now you're ready to start. The first step is identifying which areas of your life deserve a dedicated AI advisor. You'll do this in a single conversation, using pull prompting.

Do this now

Open a new chat in ChatGPT. This is your first pull session. Paste the prompt below, then just answer its questions honestly. Don't overthink, go with your first instinct on each answer.

Opening prompt

I want to build a personal AI system where you act as a dedicated advisor in different areas of my life. First, help me identify those areas. Act as a personal development coach. Ask me a few questions to understand my situation, my age, where I live, work, finances, relationships, goals, and anything else relevant. Then suggest 5 to 7 life areas where having an AI advisor with deep personal context would be genuinely useful. One area per line, with a brief reason why.

Push back on suggestions that don't feel right. Merge or rename areas until the list feels like yours, not aspirational, not generic. Write down your final list somewhere, you'll need it in the next step.

05

Interview yourself in each area

For each area on your list, you're going to open a fresh chat and let the AI interview you. One area per session, don't try to combine them. Each interview should take 20 to 40 minutes and go wherever it needs to go.

Do this for each area

Open a new chat. Paste the prompt below, replacing [AREA] and [ROLE] with the relevant values from the table underneath. Then answer every question it asks, honestly, including the uncomfortable parts. When you feel you've covered everything, say "we're done."

Interview prompt

I want you to interview me about [AREA]. Act as [ROLE]. Your goal is to understand me deeply: not just the facts, but how I think, what drives me, what I avoid, and what has shaped me. Ask one question at a time. Wait for my full answer before moving on. When I say we're done, synthesize everything into a structured profile document I can save and reuse.

Area Role to assign
Financial life Personal wealth advisor who understands financial psychology, not just numbers
Family, relationships & history Family therapist and systemic relationship counselor
Work & creative life Executive coach with experience in creative careers and transitions
Future self & learning Personal development strategist focused on unrealized potential
Food & health Integrative health coach focused on sustainable habits and energy
Language practice Language coach and conversation partner, speak to me in [language]

Include the uncomfortable parts, the contradictions, the things you avoid, the patterns you keep repeating. That's where the useful context lives. The AI doesn't judge. It just gets more useful the more specific you are.

06

Build your system prompts

At the end of each interview, when you say "we're done," use the closing prompt below to ask the AI to synthesize everything into a permanent profile document, your system prompt.

Step 1

Before writing the final document, give me a concise bullet list of the main topics and key insights from our conversation that you'll include in the system prompt. I'll confirm before you write it.

Review the bullet list. Correct anything that's missing or off. Then when you're happy with it, send this:

Step 2

Looks good. Now create the full system prompt as a PDF. Go deep. The bullet list was just a preview. Title it clearly after the area we covered.

Adapt the second prompt slightly depending on the area, a financial interview might add "include my relationship with money and risk tolerance"; a family interview might add "capture the key relationship dynamics." The AI knows what you covered. You're just nudging the focus. And remember: the PDF will be far more detailed than the bullet summary, that's the point of the two-step close.

Save it properly

Once the AI generates the document, save it to a dedicated folder on your phone or computer, something like "AI System Prompts." Label each file clearly: Financial Life System Prompt v1.pdf. You'll be uploading these into your Projects in the next step. These are version one. They will evolve. A v2, v3 will follow naturally as your life and thinking change.

07

Create a Project for each area

A Project is not just a folder. It's a persistent memory layer. You upload your system prompt PDFs into it once, and every conversation you start inside that project automatically inherits that context. You never re-explain yourself again.

Do this now, in ChatGPT

In the left sidebar, tap the pencil icon or look for New Project. Name it after one of your life areas, for example, "Financial Life." Once inside the project, look for Project Instructions or a paperclip/attachment icon, this is where you upload your system prompt PDFs. Upload the relevant files before starting any conversation. Then open your first conversation inside the project and notice: it already knows who you are.

One Project per life area. Inside each project you can have as many conversations as you want, one about a specific decision, another about a long-term question, all sharing the same foundation underneath.

Most projects benefit from having a master general profile as a common base, with the specialist profile for that area layered on top. The more context you give it upfront, the less you have to explain each time.

Don't overthink the structure upfront. Start with 3 or 4 projects. You'll reorganize naturally as you use it, merging some, splitting others. The structure is never final and that's fine.

08

Language practice

ChatGPT handles mid-conversation language switching without changing any settings. This makes it uniquely powerful for language practice, you can ask it to interview you in Chinese about something that actually matters to you, correct your mistakes in real time, then switch to German in the same thread.

The trick is loading your personal context into the language project too. That way the AI picks topics relevant to your actual life, your work, a decision you're navigating, something you're reading, rather than generic textbook scenarios. Twenty minutes of Chinese conversation about something you genuinely care about is worth more than an hour of "describe your weekend."

How to start a language session

Open your Language Practice project. Start a new conversation with something like: "Interview me in Mandarin for 20 minutes about [topic]. Correct my mistakes as we go, explain why, then continue the conversation. Stay in Chinese the whole time." Switch to voice mode for a more natural flow. When you want to shift to German, just say so mid-conversation, no settings to change.

A dedicated language project with your general profile uploaded is enough to get started. The AI already knows who you are and what you care about, it will find the right topics without you having to invent them.
